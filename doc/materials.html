Camp Materials

This is essentially a packing list of all materials necessary to run this camp.

1 Equipment

Pack the following materials:

  • 30 chromebooks

  • 30 chargers

  • 30 mice

  • 8 power strips

  • 8 extension cords

  • 5 clipboards - see below for what should be attached

  • Projector - if location doesn’t have one

  • Extra tables - if location doesn’t have enough for students + marketplace + game jam score

2.4 Schedule breakdown

Highlight the schedule breakdown below and print a copy for each instructor.

—————————-

Camp Schedule Breakdown

8:45 am - 9:15 am : Drop-off

8:45 am - 9:45 am : Intro to Camp

As they walk in:

  • Ask name – write on badge/practice kata health bar

  • Have them put bags down, sit on the floor

  • Small talk

Once everyone is there, or at 9:15:

  • Explain Kata (table) System

  • Explain Game Jam

  • Explain kata health bar, game jam health bar.

  • Get into teams (divide evenly into number of coaches) (teams between 3 and 8 students)

  • Team name and dance (each member can add a word to the team name and dance move)

  • Write team name on Daily Score butcher paper

9:45 am - 10:15 am : Table 1

Complete as many katas at this table as students can!

10:15 am - 10:45 am : Table 2

Complete as many katas at this table as students can!

10:45 am - 11:15 am : Snack/Game Break

Have snack for 10 min.

Play a game for 20 min.

  • Ninja, Wah, Human Knot, Mafia, I Like My Neighbors Who, Wax Museum, Indian Chief

  • Find these games and more here.

11:15 am - 11:45 am : Table 3

Complete as many katas at this table as students can!

11:45 am - 12:00 pm : Buffer/Get ready for lunch

Can use this time if anything took longer than expected.

Wrap-up & prep for lunch.

12:00 pm - 1:00 pm : Lunch

1:00 pm - 1:30 pm : Table 4

Complete as many katas at this table as students can!

1:30 pm - 2:00pm : Table 5

Complete as many katas at this table as students can!

2:00 pm - 2:20 pm : Snack/Game Break

Have snack for 10 min.

Play a game for 20 min.

2:20 pm - 2:30 pm : Game Jam Intro

Review goals, rules, health bar etc Bring energy up!!!

2:30 pm - 3:30 pm : Game Jam

Start without any hints.

Give them life lines: ask a coach, peak at cheat sheet.

Students can assist each other once they completed that code (no touching any other computer).

3:30 pm - 3:45 pm : Market

3:45 pm - 4:15 pm : Pick-up

2.5 Camp Jam Rules

Print out a copy of the Camp Jam Rules for each instructor.

——————————

CAMP JAM RULES

——————————

At the Beginning:

  • Be positive; encourage all students to continue and help each other

  • High focused energy; praise them once they get stuff finished/working

  • Promote focus; make sure everyone is on task and moving along

During:

  • Encourage students to:

    • Start Small, Test Often

    • Start with the 1st Kata Codes

    • After that, then customize

  • Work towards all students success

  • Find ways to praise all teams throughout the jam

    • Life Lines:

      • Life Line is asking a coach for help

      • Each student gets 1 free Life Line – once used, the coach initials the name tag

      • Each additional Life Line is $1 TSD

At the End:

  • Each student gets $10 TSD for participating!

  • Scoring:

    • 1 point per filled Health Bar Square

    • 3 points per Star Sticker

    • 5 points per Extra Life (heart sticker)

  • Emphasize Camp Total Points over team or individual scores

  • Add camp total score to Whole Camp Leader Board

2.6 Example Camp Jam code (w/definitions)

This is an example of how all the components come together during camp-jam into one piece of code:

#lang ts-camp-jam-1
(define (cool-guy)
  (custom-avatar #:sprite ))
 
(define (bad-guy)
  (custom-enemy #:sprite 
                #:ai              'easy
                #:health          200
                #:shield          100
                #:amount-in-world 5))
 
(define (sharp-stick)
  (custom-weapon #:name     "Spear"
                 #:sprite 
                 #:dart     (spear)
                 #:rarity   'common))
 
(define (metal-blade)
  (custom-weapon #:name     "Sword"
                 #:sprite 
                 #:dart     (sword)))
 
(define (paint-spitter)
  (custom-weapon #:name     "Paint Thrower"
                 #:sprite 
                 #:dart     (paint)
                 #:rarity   'epic))
 
(battle-arena-game
 #:avatar      (cool-guy)
 #:enemy-list  (list (bad-guy))
 #:weapon-list (list (sharp-stick)
                     (metal-blade)
                     (paint-spitter)))
